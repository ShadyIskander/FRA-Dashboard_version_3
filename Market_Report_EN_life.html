Insurance Market Report - Egypt

Objective

The objective of this report is to provide a comprehensive view on the insurance market in Egypt to assist the readers in making an informed decision regarding their investment and/or operation in the Egyptian Insurance market.

Target Readers

  • Executive management of Insurance companies
  • Investment bankers who may have an opportunity for their clients
  • Asset Managers seeking to understand the insurance market
  • Independent Board Directors of insurance companies

Caveats

  • Data granularity: Some data are not detailed enough for proper analytics.
  • Data integrity: Figures sometimes change between reports.
  • Imbalance between Life and Non-Life insurance.
  • Inconsistency of presentation, especially in medical insurance.

Market Evolution in 5 Years

COVID

COVID increased insurance claims costs but also raised awareness of medical insurance.

Post-COVID

A surge in business occurred due to the resumption of trade.

Devaluation

Between 2022-2024, the exchange rate increased 300%, impacting insurance costs and premiums.

Geopolitical Situation

Regional conflicts in 2024 have stressed the economy and increased risk factors.

New Insurance Law

New law introduced more coverages and specialty insurances such as medical and microinsurance.

Current Market

Penetration

Penetration rate remains at approximately 0.7% in Egypt.

Regulations

  • Regulated by the Financial Regulatory Authority.
  • Still uses Solvency 1 rather than a risk-based approach.
  • Mandatory implementation of EAS 50 (Egyptian version of IFRS 17) in 2023.

Main Players

Over 40 companies dominate the market, including Misr Insurance, Allianz, AXA, MetLife, and GIG.

Dominant Lines

  • Life: Group Life, Credit Life, and Medical Insurance.
  • Non-Life: Medical, Motor (Complementary), and Property.

Digitization

  • Companies are digitalizing operations for efficiency and fraud prevention.
  • Regulator is focusing on cybersecurity and data integration.

SWOT Analysis

Strengths

  • Growing Market Size.
  • High Population Base (young demographic).

Weaknesses

  • Lack of Public Awareness.
  • Weak Distribution Channels.
  • Fraud and Claims Management Issues.
  • Limited Affordability.
  • Product Innovation Deficiency.

Opportunities

  • Low Insurance Penetration.
  • Digital Transformation.
  • Expected economic growth due to mega projects.
  • Government Initiatives on financial inclusion.
  • Expansion into SMEs and Microinsurance.
  • International Investment Interest.

Threats

  • Economic Instability.
  • Geopolitical Vulnerability.
  • Regulatory Changes.
  • Competition from Informal Insurance.
